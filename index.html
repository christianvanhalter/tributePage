Andy Warhol

The Andy Warhol Museum, Pittsburgh, United States

    Here's a time line of Andy Warhol's life:

  • [1928] BORN
    - Born in Pittsburgh, United States. Son of Russian immigrants. Andrei Warhola, coal miner and Julia Zavacky.

  • [1937] 9 YEARS
    - He contracts an illness and for long periods in bed with his mother, who teaches him how to draw.

  • [1945] 17 YEARS
    - Study advertising illustration at the Carnegie Mellon Institute in Pittsburgh.

  • [1949] 21 YEARS
    - He makes store cabinets in Pittsburgh and travels to New York for the first time.

  • [1950] 22 YEARS
    - His illustrations appear in magazines and showcases throughout New York.

  • [1952] 24 YEARS
    - First individual exhibition.
    - His mother travels to New York to live with him.

  • [1953] 25 YEARS
    - He begins to lose his hair and begins to wear wigs.

  • [1954 - 1956] 26 - 28 YEARS
    - Publishes illustrated books.
    - The shoe company I. Miller hires him to illustrate his notices.

  • [1957 - 1958] 29 - 30 YEARS
    - Andy Warhol Enterprises sleeve to manage the orders of its customers.
    - He is one of the most sought-after illustrators of the moment.

  • [1960 - 1962] 32 - 34 YEARS
    - First works of Pop Art: Coca-Cola bottles, Campbell soup and dollars.
    - Start the series Death and disaster.
    - He works on his series Marilyn.
